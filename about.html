About

I am a researcher passionate about exploring the intersection of geospatial data, urban sustainability, and social equity. I recently completed an MSc in Geographic Data Science and Spatial Analytics at the University of Bristol, building on undergraduate training in Geographic Information Science at Jiangxi University of Science and Technology. My work combines GIS, remote sensing, and data-driven modelling to investigate how cities can become smarter, fairer, and more resilient under environmental and social pressure.

My long-term goal is to reveal and address the hidden inequalities faced by vulnerable communities in urban environments — particularly around infrastructure access, exposure to environmental risk, and resilience to extreme weather. By pairing geospatial big data with machine learning, I aim to contribute research that informs data-driven policies for sustainable and inclusive urban development.

I have also released an open-source digital book, “Geospatial Data Processing and Visualization”, built with MyST and Jupyter Book — a structured, interactive walk-through of reproducible GIS workflows and Python-based geoprocessing automation. Read the book ↗

Where I've been

A field-book of the places
that shaped this path

From a small city in Zhejiang to a data-science lab in Bristol, the digital-twin office in Beijing, and on to an Earth-science doctorate in Cardiff — five coordinates, five chapters.

01

Wenzhou

Hometown · Zhejiang, China

The coastal city where I grew up: river deltas, mountains stepping down into the sea, and a culture of restless, small-trader pragmatism. Most of my earliest curiosity about how places work—how land, water and people interlock—started here.

02

Ganzhou

2019 – 2023 · B.Sc. GIS, JXUST

Four undergraduate years in the red-soil hills of southern Jiangxi. Mornings in the GIS lab tracing rare-earth mining footprints from Landsat, afternoons biking through the old city wall. The discipline of remote sensing and Python—and a habit of asking what a map is hiding—was forged here.

03

Bristol

2023 – 2024 · MSc, University of Bristol

Across the world, into the pastel terraces and harbour fog of southwestern England. The MSc in Geographic Data Science taught me to think with code and with cities at once — spatial statistics, machine learning on satellite imagery, and long evenings debating what 'fair' means in a model.

04

Beijing

2025 – present · 51WORLD Digital Twin

Back to China, this time to the capital. At 51WORLD I work where geospatial data meets industry: city-scale digital twins, sensor fusion, and the messy politics of standing in a planning meeting and explaining what a model can — and cannot — say about a city.

05

Cardiff

Upcoming · Ph.D. Earth Sciences, Cardiff University

Back across the world to Wales, where the next chapter begins. At Cardiff University I turn toward Earth Sciences — reading the planet at the scale of deep time and living systems, and folding geospatial data and machine learning into questions about how the Earth, its climate and its people change together.
